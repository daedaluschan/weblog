# Hiring in the Age of AI Noise

As an interviewer for my team, I have recently felt a decline in the quality of candidates compared with those I met around half a year ago. I do not think the candidates suddenly became less capable. Instead, I suspect AI has made it much easier for anyone to prepare a well-fitted CV by feeding a job description into a tool and asking it to reshape their experience around that context.

<!-- more -->

That creates a much harder job for the hiring team. The first screening stage used to filter out many basic mismatches, but now a CV can look polished, relevant, and carefully aligned even when the underlying experience is not a strong fit. As a result, the responsibility for checking the basic match of skills and expectations falls more heavily onto the second stage of the process: the interview.

This feels like part of a broader era of noise. We no longer have much difficulty producing content, documents, summaries, or applications. AI can help generate them quickly and convincingly. The harder problem is finding the content that is genuinely value-added.

For hiring, that means the interview has to become more focused on evidence. Instead of only asking what someone has done, I need to understand how they think, how deeply they understand their claimed experience, and how they respond when the conversation moves away from prepared answers.

For learning, it means something similar. Learning how to use AI is important, but learning how to live in a world filled with AI-generated noise may be just as important. The new skill is not only producing more output. It is filtering, judging, questioning, and locating the real signal inside an environment where everything can look polished at first glance.
